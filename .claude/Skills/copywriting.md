# Restaurant Website — Copywriting Guide

## The Core Principle

Restaurant copy sells a feeling, not food. People book a restaurant for how they imagine they'll feel there. Write to that emotion first, describe the food second.

**Wrong:** "Vi tilbyder en bred menu med friske råvarer i hyggelige omgivelser."
**Right:** "Nogle middage husker man. PULS er stedet, hvor du ender med at bestille dessert, selv om du sagde du ikke ville."

---

## Brand Voice Framework

Before writing anything, establish the restaurant's voice across 3 dimensions:

| Dimension | Fine dining | Modern bistro | Casual/brunch |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|---------------|
| Tone | Understated, refined | Warm, confident | Playful, inviting |
| Sentence length | Short, precise | Medium, conversational | Loose, friendly |
| Humour | None | Occasional dry wit | Yes, light |
| Formality | "De" (formal) | "Du" (informal) | "Du" + slang OK |

**For Puls Kitchen og Bar:** Warm and confident. Uses "du". Modern, slightly playful but not gimmicky. "New Yorker rawness + Parisian elegance" = self-aware about its own cool factor but not pretentious about it.

---

## Hero Headline Formula

The hero headline is the most important copy on the site. It must work in 6–8 words max.

**Formula options:**

1. **The feeling:** "Middagen du har glædet dig til hele ugen."
2. **The USP:** "New Yorker råhed. Parisisk elegance. Ørestad."
3. **The invitation:** "Tag plads. Middagen venter."
4. **The contrast:** "Uformelt sted. Usædvanlig mad."
5. **The location angle:** "Ørestads hjerte banker her."

**Sub-headline:** 1–2 lines elaborating. Should contain the cuisine type + at least one emotional benefit.
Example: "Nordisk, fransk og italiensk køkken i Ørestad — præcis det sted du leder efter til brunch, frokost eller en god aften med venner."

**CTA copy:**
- Primary: "Book bord" or "Reserver din aften"
- Secondary: "Se vores menu" or "Hvad laver vi?"

## About Page Copy

This page does three things: builds trust, creates emotional connection, sets expectations.

**Structure:**
1. **Opening hook** (1 sentence) — not "Vi åbnede i [year]", but something that captures the soul: "PULS startede med en simpel idé: god mad behøver ikke et armbånd på døren."
2. **The story** (2–3 paragraphs) — founding, inspiration, location, evolution
3. **Culinary philosophy** (1 paragraph) — what drives the kitchen. Seasonal? Local suppliers? Grandmother's recipes?
4. **The team** — humanise it. Real names, real faces.
5. **Closing CTA** — short, warm: "Vi glæder os til at se dig."

**Language to avoid on About pages:**
- ❌ "Vi er passionerede om mad" (everyone says this)
- ❌ "Vi bruger kun de friskeste råvarer" (assumed)
- ❌ "Vi stræber efter at give en uforglemmelig oplevelse" (meaningless)

**Language that works:**
- ✅ Specific details: "Vi skifter menu fire gange om året — ikke fordi vi skal, men fordi vi ikke kan lade være"
- ✅ Named suppliers: "Vores oksekød kommer fra Naturkød i Jutland"
- ✅ Self-aware: "Det er ikke fine dining. Det er bedre."

## Menu Copy

**Dish name:** Clear, memorable. Not overly poetic.
- ❌ "Jordens sjæl på en tallerken"
- ✅ "Andeconfit med persillerod og vildtsauce"

**Description (1 line):** Key components + preparation method + one flavour or texture cue.
- Template: "[Main ingredient], [prep method] med [key accompaniment] og [texture/flavour note]"
- Example: "Laks, langtidsstegt med citrusurt fennikel og dild-emulsion"

**Pricing psychology:**
- Remove currency symbol where possible ("195" reads lighter than "195 kr")
- Don't right-align prices in a column — this makes users price-scan rather than dish-scan
- Use price ranges for tasting menus: "3 retter / 425" not "425,00 DKK"

**Section intros (optional):** 1 sentence per section sets the tone:
- "Brunch — Lørdag og søndag fra 10. Byg din tallerken, eller lad os gøre det for dig."
- "Middag — Fra 17.30. Bestil à la carte eller lad køkkenet vælge for dig."

## Location & Hours Copy

Hours: Don't be robotic. Add a tiny note where relevant.
```
Man–tors: 11.30–22.00
Fre–lør:  11.30–23.00   (køkkenet lukker 21.30)
Søndag:   10.00–16.00   (brunch til 15.00)
```

Directions for Ørestad specifically:
- Metro M1/M2 til Ørestad st. (5 min gang)
- Cykelparkering ved indgangen
- Gratis parkering i [P-hus navn] på hverdage efter 17

## Microcopy (the small words that matter)

**Form labels:**
- ❌ "Name" → ✅ "Dit navn"
- ❌ "Submit" → ✅ "Book bord nu" or "Send reservation"
- ❌ "Message" → ✅ "Særlige ønsker? (fødselsdag, allergi, etc.)"

**Error messages:**
- ❌ "Invalid input" → ✅ "Hov! Vi mangler din e-mail for at bekræfte bookingen."

**Confirmation:**
- ❌ "Your booking has been received" → ✅ "Vi ses! Din reservation er bekræftet til [dato] kl. [tid]. Vi glæder os."

**Empty states:**
- "Ingen ledige tider denne aften? Ring til os — vi finder en løsning."

## Danish vs English

Most Copenhagen restaurants face this choice. Rule of thumb:
- **Danish-first** if >80% of audience is Danish (neighbourhood restaurants, Ørestad locals)
- **Bilingual (DA/EN)** if tourist traffic is significant (near hotels, city centre, airport)
- **English-first** only if explicitly international (expat neighbourhoods like Nørrebro/Vesterbro, hotel restaurants)

For Ørestad: Danish primary, English secondary — proximity to airport and ITU gives international audience, but locals are the core.

**Bilingual implementation:** Keep it simple. Toggle at top right of header: `[DA] / [EN]`. Avoid auto-detect — it frustrates locals.

## SEO Copywriting Rules

1. Include the primary keyword ("restaurant ørestad") in the first 100 words of homepage body copy — naturally, not forced
2. Use H2/H3 headings that contain keywords: "Book bord i Ørestad" not just "Book bord"
3. Write meta descriptions as mini-ads: "Nordisk-fransk bistro i Ørestad. Brunch, frokost og middag ved vandet. Op til 140 gæster. Book bord online →"
4. Every image alt text should describe what's in the image + location where natural
5. Don't keyword-stuff — write for humans, optimise for machines second
